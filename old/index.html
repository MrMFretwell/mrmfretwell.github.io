Intro

My name is Martin, better known by my Online alias MrMFretwell, or "Fret" for short. I stream nightly on twitch.tv. I have currently been streaming for over 500 consecutive days focusing on variety laid back gaming, featuring both new and modern games, as well as a segment of old retro titles using real hardware. By the way, check out my content.

Streaming is my passion, and I love sharing my journeys through games together with my friends. My website is designed to provide a means to contact me, provide useful links to questions you may have about my stream and for you to learn a little bit more about me if your interested.

Retro Streaming

One of the major on-going projects on my stream is that of retro streaming. Taking old consoles and games and converting them into a format that can be processed by todays capture cards. Each console slightly varies, but generally, I try to aim for RGB SCART cables, depending on modification options available that then go to the XRGB-Mini Framemeister, an upscale unit designed to take old formats including RGB, Component, Composite and other 240p and 480p sources and upscale them pixel perfectly to 720p or 1080p and convert the signal to a digital HDMI format. I then take the HDMI signal and send it into my Avermedia C985 internal capture card for capturing within OBS Studio. With audio, I include a breakout in the HDMI line to reduce input delay in audio and take advantage of the audio jacks on my streaming machine to feed the audio in as a microphone source. If you'd like more in-depth knowledge about retro streaming, feel free ask me on the contact page, or in any of the other usual places. Alternatively YouTube and Google can provide good results, with some of my favorite resources being "mylifeingaming" on YouTube, Shmups Forum and RetroRGB.com. One common question that comes up a lot to me when it comes to retro streaming is why I don't use emulators. The truth is, I just prefer the original authentic hardware rather than simulating the experience. Emulators also have issues of their own that can make them less reliable including graphical bugs and incompatibility issues with specific games. That said I do understand that this is by far the easiest way to access retro games in the world today, and I don't judge anyone for using them. I do occasionally use them myself for testing certain aspects when streaming or to compare results I might be getting on hardware.

About

I am a 21 year old content producer from the Midlands, Great Britain. I have been producing content since late 2011. Starting with YouTube videos, then later moving into Twitch livestreaming.

I have always been around technology, and it quickly became my passion, from age 4 I was playing on the Sony Playstation One my parents bought me.Around the same time, I also began learning how to use a computer with my granddads Windows ME (gulp!) machine. Since then I've always been an eager learner when it comes to computers, and also gotten into computer games and sharing my adventures with friends online using my technology skills.

YouTube

Content production first became a hobby of mine in 2011 after seeing other "YouTubers" produce their own videos, in particular Coestar and paulsoaresjr. I began to produce "Minecraft" videos following my adventures in game, I chose to do a relaxed documentary style of video, without any significant editing pre-planned or any sort of "scripted show". Though I did try to add some editing flair here and there. The highlight of this was the launch of my long running "Let's Play" series MrMFretwell Plays Minecraft in 2013. Spanning over two hundred episodes it documented my every move in game, and covered most of the stories going on in my life. Eventually times moved on and things changed for me. I lost interest in Minecraft with it being the only game I had played for so long, along with my mental health deteriorating I chose to end the series, and all single player content on my YouTube channel, and instead chose to focus on my newly launched Twitch channel.

Twitch

With the end of my YouTube content I chose to focus on Twitch streaming, which gave me a free outlet to express myself more naturally and for longer periods. With inspiration from Coestar I began the #StreamADay challenge. In many ways, myself and Coe have walked a similar path and it has lead us to even cross at certain intervals. My goals for streaming began as simply to stream every day and to stay productive, while I figured other things out. Since then I have streamed consecutively every single night since the 22nd of October 2015 now completing more than 500 days. Streaming gave me a creative outlet and I stayed productive, this had a hugely positive impact on my mental health, reducing the effects of depression and meeting a number of awesome people along the way.
